In the sense that magic serves as a metaphor...sort of, yes. That's the gist. If I were to stray further into 'people stop listening to me here' territory, I'd say that every method of understanding is a metaphor, an abstraction from reality that serves as a useful mechanism. The idea of an 'atom' is a very useful metaphor. We never get to reality. The idea that I try to push, is that methods of understanding outside of the one we currently think of as 'real' can also be useful in approximating an understanding.

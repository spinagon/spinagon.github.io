Well that's fascinating, that this is the artistic 'result' of that challenge. If I had to read into it I'd say that the figures represent darkness and Otherness- animals have always been a motif that signals a bit of Otherness -and their presence here on the ceiling is indicative of the fact that Kennet-to-come is a future, imminent but not 'here'. The white ribbons from the family man- representing normal society, purity culture, and the customs and values therein -aren't discarded or useless, but they're also not wasted covering up dicks and buttholes; instead they're used to place necessary restrictions on the action taking place.

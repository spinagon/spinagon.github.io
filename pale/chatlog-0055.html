tw: mention of suicide; nothing graphic. no spoilers for anything, just thought I'd make reading voluntary. So in some of the worst parts of my admittedly-short life (ages 11-15), I never seriously considered suicide (needless to say, lots of my friends did; I grew up with lots of queer, mentally ill, or otherwise marginalized people). This is because, early on, I'd developed a coping mechanism that wouldn't become obvious to me until I hit college and found people using the same mitigating techniques with a little more intentionality. Whenever my family would drive up to reno or davis or really anywhere I thought was cool enough, or had a particularly interesting/sheltering public environment (such as abandoned train tunnels) I would write down addresses and remember those places- in case I needed to hop on my bike with minimal supplies and run away from home. I set aside cash, I looked up the bike routes and how long they would take, I planned on snacks to bring, places to refuel, etc. The public school system was awful for me- even after I obtained an IEP, teachers wouldn't read it, would still refuse me accommodations/accuse me of cheating, etc. But whenever it got particularly bad, I would think about hopping on my bike, grabbing my snacks and my cash and living in some decommissioned train tunnels in the mountains. My mind was trained to think of that- frankly, delusional -plan long before thinking of dying. I'd built myself a safety net so that I could fantasize about not dealing with my problems in a way that didn't endanger my life...as much. The guy who told me abt this coping mechanism had to build it more intentionally, training himself every day to jump to "I'll drop out of school" as an immediate response. Would running away have actually fixed any of my problems? Fucking no. It was a delusional thought. But one that- I now realize -got me through a whole lot of shit.

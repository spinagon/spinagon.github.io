Someone please explain to the Negateers that praxis produces ideology, as much as the other way around. But no seriously, I really love what this chapter does to demonstrate how the methods with which revolutions handle internal conflict can lead to moral death spirals. Because if you execute allies for being insufficiently enthusiastic about committing atrocities on kids, this will be part of the development of your ideology. You will find yourself in a situation where you are unable to deescalate even when it's advantageous and atrocities for atrocities' sake, even if no one can explain why it's necessary, will become part of your operation. Because people know that if they suggest deescalation, it might get them stabbed in the back and killed. And this will then become part of your ideology, because few people like thinking about how they just signed off on, or maybe even committed, crimes against humanity. And on an even more basic level than that, people just prefer to come up with moral reasoning for their actions than to think of them as forced moves. Especially revolutionaries who think of themselves as the ones controlling the shape the world is going to take. You will come up with reasons why the atrocities are important. Your ideology will work to reflect that, to become an ideology which demands atrocity. Wildbow is just so damn good about showing how Charles' movement got here, and how it's going to keep getting worse, and I love it. (edited)

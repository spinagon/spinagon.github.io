but first of course, some observations my partner and I had about the court emblems, because we had a conversation and they're an art major and it was fun: -No emblem is symmetrical except for Winter- even High Spring just gives us the illusion of symmetry. Winter is also built of repeating patterns which is extremely fitting, seeing as all Winter fae are repeating patterns -my partner was able to identify the butterfly in High Fall's court as a poisonous one. -High Summer's rose has no thorns, indicative of their general style. -High/Low contrasts are frequent and fun. -The Fall courts reflect each other, with the negative space in one mapping to the positive space of the other, and then the eye in the middle for each. -The Springs involve sun imagery and cities (although low spring also looks like an eye- especially with the trees suggesting veins -which gives it synergy with the fall courts)- Low Spring also paints in greys, suggesting a more fluid morality and less binary (also suggested by the fact that there is no 'up' in low spring, whereas High Spring is quite directional). -Summer fits this least- there's the recurring beat of the growth, the rose without thorns above, and just thorns below, and then the sword's shape mapping to the shape of the flag being flown -but it also fits for Low Summer (being further from 'true fae') to be less cohesive with the set.

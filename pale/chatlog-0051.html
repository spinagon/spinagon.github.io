JayManiac 11/26/2020 5:36 PM
I hadn't thought about that, actually- in the characterization metaphor, I mostly focused on the things about the ritual that were different for each person to go through it, but when it comes its more universal effects, I suppose I see it mostly as 'I will play by a certain set of expectations, and subvert you within them, so that the resolution of conflicts will be satisfying'. Every story has to establish its rules, even in 'realistic' fiction, and as a character is introduced they have to demonstrate a capability to follow those rules, to play along- even if it's just the art style of an animated tv-show. This is as important as the demonstration of unique traits and intrigue: a part of character design is those features and values and a solid silhouette, but a part of it is also adapting/creating that character in a way that plays along with everything else you've got.

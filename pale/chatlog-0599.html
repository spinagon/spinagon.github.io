No Spirits in here because they're satisfied the Pillars are handling it. Pillars manifested in very specific ways. And when those specific ways are disrupted, carefully and deliberately and completely, all that's left is the Self. And the Self, usually limited by external perception, by management by other forces, replaces all of that with visceral flesh. I'm thinking of the way that when you go into a dark room and stare at your faint reflection for long enough, it begins to warp and twist. Doubly so if you're on hallucinogens of any sort. The Self is inherently mutable, and when the usual things that constrain it - Incarnate things, spiritual things, connections to other people, Realmic influences - well, those things exist for a reason. Without them, or with them sufficiently scrambled that they can't act, the Self takes over. So to bring that back to Jay's conception, being a Horror is the experience of being a fundamental force, to a greater or lesser degree. Horror practice is exploiting the benefits of using your Self as a fundamental, unconstrained force without taking on the responsibilities, and then finding some other poor patsy to take on the responsibility without any of the benefit.

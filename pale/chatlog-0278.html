Once, long ago, a man caught and bound a cunning leprechaun who'd been ceaselessly playing tricks on him. The man demanded, of course, to be taken to the leprechaun's stash of gold (a feature which all leprechauns possess) and having little choice in the matter, the leprechaun agreed. He lead the man through the forest, under bridges and through tunnels and into a place half in fairy-land where finally he stopped at a field of hundreds and hundreds of rocks. The leprechaun pointed to a rock twice the man's size and said "my treasure is kept under there, though even I cannot extract it, and required the help of a giant to hide it in the first place!" The man for many minutes tried to move the rock, tying ropes to it and dragging, trying to lever it with a stick, but to no avail. "Fine," he said. "You've fulfilled your end of the bargain, I'm off to retrieve my friends so we can move this rock and get to the treasure" Having little choice, the leprechaun agreed, but something in his smile gave the man pause. "Ahh, I see, you're hoping I'll forget which rock it is, and leave you with your treasure!" he said, for all the rocks did truly look similar, and it was a great big rock field. "So before you go, I'll tie this rainbow handkerchief to the correct rock, and you must promise not to move it, or I'll never release you." The leprechaun pouted, and whined, but agreed that he wouldn't move the ribbon, nor would he compel any other creature to move it in his place. And so the man went back and retrieved a band of twenty, promising each a cut of the treasure as long as they helped him move the rock...but of course, when they got there, 'round every rock in the field was tied an identical rainbow handkerchief. And though they toiled for hours digging up the rock field, they never found the treasure.

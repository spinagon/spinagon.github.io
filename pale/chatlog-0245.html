JayManiac 7/21/2021 9:56 PM
Yeah, I think upon reflection I do disagree with myself at the beginning of the story. I've always been wary of the idea of tradition and older ways of doing things- I have a knee-jerk reaction to because of the arguments that tend to invoke it -so when I saw the Kenneteers choose the old way over the new way with seemingly no consideration I was worried that they would stick too tightly to what other practitioners called 'tradition'. Now I think I see that my timeline was way off, I wasn't thinking in the correct scale. When people use the 'tradition' argument, they're typically attempting to justify the way the system is, the capitalism, christianity, and colonization which define the modern day practice. But of course, in the scheme of human history these are all fairly new ideas: capitalism is only around 500 years old, and if Miss is working on a timescale that includes the Seal of Solomon almost 3,000 years past, then her 'old ways' are very different. Analyzing it as wood vs metal- rather than old vs new -of course wood is the way to go, it's the blindingly obvious choice once I get past that hurdle. Of course metal is popularized and fundamentalized and mass produced by capitalism, by burning fossil fuels, by all the unsustainable practices that leach the world of its resources and destroy the land that provides for them. Wood is about the older ways than that, a tradition in which humankind is aligned with nature, in which it sees life as an element of the universe. It's a more sustainable choice, and by reaching back to it maybe we can heal the practices of today.

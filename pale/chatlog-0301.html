It wasn’t actually on a cliff. It was heavily spirit, of the spirit world, and the cliff’s edge in this metaphor was reality. Every organ and every part of it that was anchored into reality was a handhold. The more handholds it had, the easier it was to get more, to pull the bulk of itself into reality here. But, John knew, there was a cost. In this metaphor, the titan at the cliff’s edge still needed to expend energy to lift a hand, to get a hand settled into position at the edge, so he could pull himself up. Actually lifting his own weight up would be a task that got harder, not easier, in proportion with his size. Repeatedly forcing that movement, denying the ability to get over the edge… John hoped to exhaust it. To drain its strength by forcing it to repeatedly try to get a grip on reality and then lose that grip.Anyways, we're jumping right back into the fight and John's strategic mind is a very fun place to be as he fights something that doesn't quite make sense for a soldier to be confronting. He has to take another route, work it into a more physical metaphor of handholds, slipping, energy expended in effort so that he can figure out the best tactics with which to defeat it. It's fun because he does have a robust understanding of how spirits work, and can very effectively work them into a different framework (that still fits my preferred style to read fights in). If I had the time to write stories at this point I'd definitely have to come back to John's chapters for fight-writing tricks.

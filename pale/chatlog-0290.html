Basically- and I know this is delaying the chapter read, sorry -back in Olden Times n shit, every region of people had their own ways of practicing magic, with their own hierarchies and their own shit going on. Corvidae was brought forth by people who became 'practitioners' of magic only when they were intoxicated by a specific substance in a ritual, for example. People had their own conceptions of Others, their own forms of innocence and interaction with the magical and so on- in some sense there was no concept of 'magic' as opposed to 'innocence' and it was just one more thing that was an aspect of people's lives (like sex, farming, etc).
4:35 PM
This essentially 'limits' the power of Others and Practitioners, because the only sources of belief and structure and power are the people in a given area. Solomon's demons were only as great as the people who believed in them (which is how he was able to conquer them in the first place).
4:37 PM
But then the Seal comes along and starts imposing itself more strictly on everybody else, marching along and being pushed into spaces that it wasn't built for, forcing the Others in those places to abide by its rules. The more land it conquers, the more people give it power, and the more powerful its extreme ends (demons and angels) become, until they're so immense that the strongest of them are basically unconquerable by any individuals.

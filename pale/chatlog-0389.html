Magic in the Otherverse all has Karma underlying it, determining the most efficient aspects of what it can be, and curses are the most blatant example: you articulate a sense of 'justice' in the curse and argue to the universe why this suffering is justified to incur, you set a challenge that can theoretically be cleared to alleviate it. Curses are often some bullshit that people don't deserve, just like- for example -prison sentences, because Karma is so broken and warped and twistable as the hand of the system. So what Lucy is trying to do- both with curses and with karma at large -is set sanctions on it and turn it into a system that works for the Good. Curse practitioners are Karmic Law practitioners in some sense, and Lucy is leaning into that in her own way, making this hugely variable practice her method for keeping Law and doing Good. Which is fantastic when you think of where she was at the start of the story and the impulses she sometimes has trouble controlling. Lucy has to struggle with the parts of the system in herself that still believe in punitive or otherwise unhelpful versions of justice. It's a really fun way to go, and I'm glad I was able to find the common thread of these things. The curses are also fun, I'm just not pulling them because all I'd have to say on the matter is 'oo look, fun curse'. I will be keeping track of the general values she seems to be reinforcing, or where I might disagree with her assessment of what's 'right' in a curse. I don't know why I hadn't put together that curses were a kind of weapon and tool that had their own moral implications every time they were used.

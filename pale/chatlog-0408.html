Well that's fascinating, that this is the artistic 'result' of that challenge. If I had to read into it I'd say that the figures represent darkness and Otherness- animals have always been a motif that signals a bit of Otherness -and their presence here on the ceiling is indicative of the fact that Kennet-to-come is a future, imminent but not 'here'. The animal-head thing is also specifically something the Kenneteers invoked as a mark of their status as Wild Practitioners- a mask and a Self that runs deeper, and suggests an Otherness that's apart from the current system of the practice. The part I'm most confident about- and my favorite part -is how the nudity is handled and what that represents. Nudity is something that's considered uncouth by society without necessarily being bad- just like a lot of the stuff Verona embraces -and this becomes a place where that can be displayed without issue or without calling attention to it. The white ribbons from the family man- representing normal society, purity culture, and the customs and values therein -aren't discarded or useless, but they're also not wasted covering up dicks and buttholes; instead they're used to place necessary restrictions on the action taking place.

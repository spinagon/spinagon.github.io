In Cultural Materialist Anthropology, the idea is that societies form first (causally) on the basis of behavioral Infratructure: how do they relate to their environment with regard to Production and Reproduction (very basics of staying alive). The infrastructure developed is related deterministically to the Structure: ethics, behavioral domestic and political economies, where power is consolidated in every layer of government and how material affairs and exchanges are handled. This structure relates deterministically to Superstructure: cognative goals, values of a society, rules, taboos, plans, philosophies, beliefs, religion, symbolism and ideas, arts, rituals, games, science. The idea behind Cultural Materialism is that the material conditions will determine infrastructure will determine structure will determine superstructure. Which seems fairly rational, until you encounter Superstructural feedback: people doing things that make zero sense in terms of material conditions, infrastructure or structure, having worked top down from Superstructure. It happened so often that many scholars have since tried to redevelop the whole concept, though it still remains an interesting anthropological tool.

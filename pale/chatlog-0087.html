I think it’s okay though,” Lucy said, quiet.As an aside before I head out for the day: I think I've stumbled onto a reason I really adore urban fantasy here, and it may be one that I've expressed before. Kids deal with a whole lot of shit. Their world is full of imagination, of rivalries and social orders and problems that they're utterly convinced that an adult cannot understand. And most of the time? They're kind of right. When adults get involved they infantalize the problem, or suggest solutions that- for reasons the kids can feel in their bones -just will not work, and will only make problems worse. Because bringing in an incredibly self-righteous and powerful individual with zero understanding of what's going on is not the way to handle a delicate situation, and kids are well aware of this. they're not here to let the things they value so highly be belittled and dismissed by adults, so they mostly keep it to themselves, open up more to their friends who can actually understand what's going on. This is Urban Fantasy (not quite the right word, as 'shadowhunters' is excluded and 'stranger things' and 'aliens in the attic' are included, so it's a genre that overlaps with urban fantasy). This is what kids understand without really understanding what they understand: they live in a magical world. Its rules resemble those of the 'real' world, but not always in ways that make sense. What happens there is vitally important, but likely to be belittled or not taken seriously by those whose heads are stuffed firmly into the great and impenetrable veil that is their ass. It's very rare that these adults- typically unknowingly -help, and often the rules and the structures they attempt to impose will be devastating to the outcomes of said delicate situations.

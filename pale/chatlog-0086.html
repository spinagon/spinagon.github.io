Okay before I dive into the extra materials and at least start on the chapter, here's a mini fae courts rant that someone else has probably done already because I'm super behind on the discourse and we have a lot of well-read individuals around the doof audience. So excluding Winter, the three seasons form a trio, and if I know anything about trios imagined after 375 BC- especially trios that have to do with government and society -it's that most of them are going to bear some resemblance to Plato's theory of the Tripartite Soul. For those who don't know, in Republic- Plato's dialectic on the definition of justice and also what perfect society looks like -he creates the analogy of a person to a city (yes, it's basically inside out) and divides its people into 3 parts. The Appetitive Soul is that which wants, that which consumes and that which drives all our greed, hunger, etc. It's the love of stuff and the love of getting stuff, and it's represented by the merchant class of the city, and represented as a monstrous black horse. The Spirited Soul is that which propels, creating passion and anger and drive to do our best. It's the love of self and of those we identify with, and the instinct to defend them. Plato equates it with the military class of the city, the warriors, and it's often represented by a beautiful white stallion. And finally the logos or The Rational Soul is that which thinks, that which measures and speaks and learns. It's the love of knowledge, truth, and dialect. Plato argues that this aspect should command the other two and must always keep them in check in order to live a just existence. This is where we get the idea of philosopher kings.
8:15 PM
Everyone after that in history basically flipped their shit and thought plato was a genius apparently because this tripartite model is everywhere. You can find certain elements of it in the depictions of the christian god, it's been applied to all kinds of mythos retroactively, and it's the basis (arguably) for The Chariot tarot card (philosopher king in the center with the black and white beasts at his command; the three souls were in fact euqated a chariot with horses and driver at some point during the analogy), just to give a few examples.
8:16 PM
Anytime people have tried to figure out people (through psychology or storytelling) or how a society should function (government and ethics) this model has entered or started the conversation. For example: American government's three branches- legislative, executive, and judicial -map to the three souls and their roles (the court gets the final say, as the logical part, while the executive branch 'defends' and the legislative branch manages the wants and needs of the people, ideally). But the example that really helps us here is the psychological one. The tripartite soul informs Freud's idea of the Id- the appetitive base desires -the Ego- the spirited defender/mediator -and the Superego- the values, morals, and methods of thought imparted by the society you live in, your 'logos'.
8:16 PM
So the three seasons represented by the fae courts map similarly: high society and aristocracy tracks with the superego, the 'rational' soul, the logos. It's created by association, by society as a whole as opposed to individuals, which tracks in terms of the spring courts- especially high spring -interacting much less with humanity, and more with fae. The ego, the spirited defender is the High Summer, the passion, the rage and the romantic aspirations. Which leaves the id, the base desires, the merchant class of the analogy, to the Fall courts, the ones who interact the most with individual humans, focused on exchange and individual narratives.

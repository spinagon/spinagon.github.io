I'm saying that we're talking about two different things. You're talking about the amount of wealth. I'm talking about control over how that wealth is distributed. So let's take the example of Augustus Caesar. Over the course of his life, he had the power of life and death over most of the people in the Roman Empire. He used the power of proscription at various times to declare people Enemies of Rome, sentence them to death, and confiscate all their assets. A vast amount of the political power in Rome was concentrated in him. Now compare the modern U.S. I think we can agree the the "pie" is much larger in the modern U.S. More wealth, more comfort, etc. Power has been concentrating upwards toward the already powerful in recent years, but no one in the U.S. has that as much control over resources as Augustus did. As of this writing, no single person, nor I think the government as a whole, can simply put up a Death List and start confiscating things. Power of control over the (larger) pie is spread out over a more diverse population.

I agree with the heart of what these guys were doing, and it's really interesting to see them looking back on their 20 year old selves- who were bombing buildings, being regularly targeted by the fbi, etc. -with a wide range of reactions from 'we were stupid kids' to 'i still believe we were doing the right thing'. I can't really blame them. There were problems with what they did, absolutely, and some of their fellow leftists were quick to point those out- the black panthers in particular had a bit to say about the WUO, how 'dragging their people out to be massacred' is not an effective form of revolution -but they were trying to help. They just didn't have the experience of navigating those political tensions that the Black Panthers and other such orgs did because...most of the WUO members were exceedingly privileged white college students. And it's not as if they didn't realize that either, they were constantly pointing out instances of privilege and using the idea of privilege as a motivator to continue enduring- for example -the constant harassments by the FBI. There was just...something. Maybe an element of self-importance and individualist conquest to their revolution, that put them on a track that got a lot of them killed, and the rest washed up.

Yeah, it's true that this doesn't exactly work for the 'big project' type things, it's not going to set you on-course for whatever you want to do long-term because...only you can decide stuff about that. Creativity from thinking critically about media also isn't exactly covered, but I still think that there are lots of elements you would want in a good starting point for creativity. Lots of the point of the first exercise (which I'm not sure if I conveyed in the video itself) was 'allow yourself to have ideas, even bad ones', in a similar vein to things you've said surrounding the web-serial format. You obviously came at creativity from a very different place, but some of the keys to creative flow that you've talked about share similarities. I was approached about this by a very prolific visual artist who wanted to write comics (even just newspaper comics) but found himself dealing with a lot of self-doubt and lack of ideas, and this video was born from a very refined version of the advice I tried to give him. It's also something I can use for much the same purpose, even if I know a bit of what I want to be doing creatively. For me, writer's block is still very much a thing, and I get past it with these questions and lists, using them as warmups before I know I need to write, create, or plan something. It gets my head in the mindset of thinking critically and seeing connections, it typically turns out as a bit of a confidence boost for what I need to do moving forwards (seeing that even if I don't hit the mark the first few times I'm still capable of hitting it), and I think those are all things that are valuable to someone trying to be creative, whether it's their first time or their four-hundredth.

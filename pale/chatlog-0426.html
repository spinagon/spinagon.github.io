I think part of the reason I felt strongly about certain issues raised here is that a lot of the way I navigate the world is keyed for...well, spaces in which Verona would thrive. Lots of my friends have complicated approaches to sexuality and romantic orientation, whether it's being ace/aro or just being other forms of queer, so in my circles when we run into problems with romance/sexuality/attraction/jealousy a lot of the solution becomes re-examining whether biases we've been raised with are interfering with the life we want to be living. And because a lot of the language around this sort of thing is via a social script that doesn't exactly fit us (like, if the script has different roles for boys and girls, then the 2/3rds of my friend group that's nonbinary is hosed), I think I've become hyperattuned to when people (including me) are appealing to greater structures to justify themselves or explain their actions rather than expressing their specific needs and wants (like Jeremy does when he says 'it wouldn't be fair to caroline') because that specific and open expression is really important to reaching a place where everyone is comfortable and understands each other. Trust and open communication are essential to keeping anything running around here, because when you can't follow a script you have to address problems and needs individually, approaching it from the perspective of 'this situation between people is unique, and the solution might be something that wouldn't work for anyone else'.

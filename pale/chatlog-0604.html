And if the hole was left unplugged, then just like many realms have echoes of reality or rely on reality, reality would thusly have echoes of that ontologically-greater realm. We would likely know it by a name, for its effect on the practice would be observable. I can think of four distinct possibilities for this:
  • There is a kind of Other which have maintained their own society and yet continued to interact with Reality, becoming known to it. They exist in the story, their powers are coveted by practitioners and seen as immensely desireable and dangerous. However, while they may not be lacking in ontological power or- perhaps -even complexity, they lack novelty, and thus their independence comes with a side effect: Winter. The Winter Court poses relatively little danger to Reality because it is so controlled, and thus the contest between the two worlds never has to happen. I would say that the Courts rely more on reality than the other way around.
-The demon realms could be this ontological power which nothing in reality can even hope to stand up against. I don't think they are, because demons certainly seem to be central to the Seal of Solomon, which is what defines Reality in the first place (I expect them to be more a symptom of the Seal than an exception) but from a perspective of absolute power....this is what we'd be looking for. -Primevals may in fact be more vast, both physically and ontologically, than Reality. Those horrors may have their own spaces which become very dangerous when the line between worlds is breached. Judges and other agents of the Seal are often capable only of keeping these beings out, not often of affecting their spaces, bringing them into Reality's fold, or appropriating their power.

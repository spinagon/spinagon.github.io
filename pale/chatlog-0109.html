In the text Umbrage, on Scourges: The Demesne forms an easy bridge between the fleeting world of man and our bent and churning dark at the heart of reality… And, though the citations are abundant, we can finish our illustrations with a quote from the apocryphal Art Almighty: …we contend the Manse [Demesne] is not only an art we may practise, but one we must. It is proven and enshrined that the Manse stands above the Kingdom of Man and below the Kingdom of God. To elevate a place to it is to raise ourselves and raise this world closer to Him… The Demesne, paradoxically, is one fixed location in our reality, but a roaming place in our cosmology, capable of being adjacent or connected to any realm.In any case, that whole goblin and fae schpeel isn't at all what we're supposed to be talking about here so let's actually get into that, shall we? I think the most interesting passage here comes from the Art Almighty, because one, it takes this concept and shows us how one particular culture/set of practitioners has applied spiritual meaning to it which is really really fun, because of course the demesne is versatile enough to allow that. But also it reveals something akin to what Max Weber was trying to tell us in The Protestant Ethic and the Spirit of Capitalism: that we've elevated the idea of property to spiritual significance, paving the way for such justifications as 'manifest destiny' when we go to a place and start taking everything. It brings us closer to God, so how could it be a total dick move!?

JayManiac 1/13/2023 2:13 AM
I still have a couple problems with this wellspring/processing interpretation in general, and even though I really like it I don't quite think it's accurate.
2:13 AM
First of all we've got to understand that the realms we know of do not hold true as the cosmological constructions of all reality. Some places don't have the Ruins underlying them, some may not have access to the Warrens or Faerie or Abyss, with the functions of those realms taken up by other ones with different qualities. To put it another way: the cosmology shifts from place to place. And I don't think those functions are always the same ones from place to place either. The warrens may be more processing than wellspring or vice versa depending on the area, and goblins are versatile enough that they can cover a wide variety of functions. While we might think of them as the fundamental realms, they all came into being in different ways and are maintained in different ways. None are absolutely essential or omnipresent (excepting perhaps Spirit, and maybe the Paths, which we'll get to) and I believe the roles they play are slightly more nuanced.
2:15 AM
The Paths just might be more omnipresent. Or at least, they seem to exist in relation to being untethered from Reality, in a dichotomy with it, so it's feasible that they could exist everywhere. I'm not sure that the same is true of Faerie. So in places where the Paths can be accessed but Faerie cannot, does the wellspring persist as a fundamental truth? Is Faerie merely the force in command of this always-accessible wellspring that is narrative novelty? I think not. The Faerie Courts are more complex than that.

I suppose I'll start by saying I have a hard time pinning down what manipulation actually is, because I don't understand how it's different from people management. I've come to the conclusion that manipulation is bad, and people management is neutral. I'm fairly articulate and think in ways that tend to resonate with people; I have a higher chance of just getting people to agree with what I say merely by talking. Does that mean I'm being manipulative? Most of it depends on my intent. Some of it might depend on my tactics, but if I'm talking to my friend and making jokes that I think she'll find amusing to lighten the mood of an otherwise difficult conversation, is that manipulation? Does it depend on whether I'm aware of what I'm doing? Isn't that sort of a bad metric that also demonizes people who have to get to these normative ways of interacting through roundabout ways (such as autistic people, or people who are speaking a second language very well)? I have such a hard time defining this sort of thing that I just spent a good while rambling getting nothing done so let's dig into the real meat of the discussion and give up on trying to frame it.
5:43 PM
Let's for all intents and purposes in this discussion assume that Kennet has to Awaken someone and that it's the best solution to their problem- and also that to make the change she needs to Miss needs to have practitioners who can be advocates for Others and for Kennet. Not something the story treats as given- since Miss did let the Carmine die -but something it's easy to get lost in the weeds with because of the complexity of this world; there are lots of ways to make change and I think it's safe to say this is a really effective one as examples go. Now, how do you go about that in a way that is not manipulative, keeping in mind the protections of innocence getting in the way?
5:43 PM
Well you would need to pick people with a good balance of openness to new ideas and a strong enough sense of self that they won't be easily manipulated. I think the Kenneteers qualify- Avery has a strong conscience, Lucy is especially attuned to bullshitting and isn't especially trusting, and Verona has trauma responses that immunize her to many manipulation tactics. If Miss had been looking for people with the intent of manipulation, I'm sure she would've found them. I think the fact that Miss was the Kenneteers prime suspect at the start is evidence that she did a good job at not being manipulative. Another danger of manipulation in this situation is the fact that you can isolate someone and become the sole influence on them- hell, Avery's practically primed for that sort of manipulation at the beginning of the story. But Miss picks them as a group, gives them people they can trust and compare notes with- it's yet another tactic to prevent potential manipulation. And for the final tactic that Miss employs: she actively wants their input on things, and is willing to let the Kenneteers make the decisions, influence the plan. She's not trying to get them to think one particular thing that remains unchanging, she's letting them have input on the development of the idea and encouraging that path. A lot of what defines what we call manipulation seems to be imbalance. I may be telling jokes to lighten the mood of a conversation with my friend, but she's also leaving deliberate pauses so that I can elaborate on things and come to her conclusions. Manipulation in turn. I think the fact that Miss is currently hurtling towards Kennet for Verona's idea is good evidence that she wants the influence to flow both ways.

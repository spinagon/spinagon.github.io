I think a whole lot of people don't really connect with the idea of traditional romance? Or at least would be better suited by some other system. I've talked with a lot of people on the asexual and aromantic spectrums, lots of neurodivergent people, and just queer people in general about this and it seems to be a recurring topic. Neurodivergent people, for instance, don't always think in the way that maps to systems built by neurotypicals. All the subtle social cues and intricacies that people have constructed within this idea of romance take so much mental energy for some of us to process and keep track of, which can make communication with a partner really agonizing if you don't find some other more optimal system for doing so. Aromantic/asexual people...well that's pretty self explanatory, and by that I mean that I could explain it for hours and you would understand exactly the same amount about how the fuck we process romance and attraction as we do (ok some of us maybe have ascended and figured themselves out, but I sure as heck haven't). I think those of us who are in healthy relationships within these groups (or really just...anywhere outside of the group of 'people who don't vibe w/ traditional romance') have to break the system in some way, even if it's not an obvious way like open relationships/polyamory.

In Japan, relationships range from the Casual to a standardized variant on the familiar, where the practitioner opens up several spaces and binds two or three familiars at once. This dynamic is tyrannical on the part of the practitioner, who assumes shrewd control, but among the Others it is often parallel or complementary, with Others balancing one another out and absorbing the brunt of effects for one another. The process is somewhat fragile, and the nature of the practitioner’s relationship to the familiars resembles a chess player with his pieces; if he topples, all tied to him suffer.

A small, tangentially-related note: Elliot often says that the spirits map to psychology, and I've always had a bit of a problem with that...but I've recently realized my problem with that statement is also my problem with the field of psychology as a whole, which is that I'm a post-structuralist. A lot of the classic studies in psychology- including the famous 'Marshmallow Experiment' -are failing to replicate results in modern times. You could say that now we're better at accounting for variables, and since our methods improve our current results are more 'accurate'....but the way I see it, those original experiments were done in a different world, a different culture, of course they're not replicating. Our society shapes how we think to a degree that I don't think psychology is even close to appreciating- especially not when the majority of psychology research that we measure new studies against and corroborate results with is performed on American college students, which is an extremely biased selection of people.
